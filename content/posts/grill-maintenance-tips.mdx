# Grill Maintenance 101: Keep Your BBQ in Top Shape

A well-maintained grill performs better, lasts longer, and keeps your food safe. Here's your complete guide to grill care.

## After Every Use

### The 15-Minute Routine

1. **Burn off residue** (5 minutes)
   - Close lid, turn to high
   - Let it heat for 5 minutes
   - This carbonizes stuck-on food

2. **Brush the grates** (3 minutes)
   - Use a quality grill brush
   - Scrub while grates are hot
   - Work in direction of grate bars

3. **Empty drip pan** (2 minutes)
   - Prevents grease fires
   - Reduces smoke and flare-ups
   - Makes deep cleaning easier

4. **Wipe exterior** (2 minutes)
   - Remove any splatters
   - Check for grease buildup
   - Keep handles clean

5. **Cover when cool** (3 minutes)
   - Wait until completely cool
   - Use a quality grill cover
   - Protects from elements

## Deep Cleaning Guide

### What You'll Need
- Grill brush
- Putty knife or scraper
- Bucket of warm soapy water
- Sponges or rags
- Paper towels
- Aluminum foil
- Cooking oil spray

### Gas Grill Deep Clean

**Step 1: Disconnect and disassemble**
- Turn off gas at tank
- Remove grates, heat plates, burners

**Step 2: Soak grates**
- Place in large tub or garbage bag
- Cover with warm soapy water
- Let soak 15-30 minutes

**Step 3: Clean interior**
- Scrape inside of firebox
- Remove debris into drip pan
- Wipe with soapy water

**Step 4: Clean burners**
- Use brush to clear ports
- Check for spider webs/nests
- Inspect for damage

**Step 5: Clean grates**
- Scrub with brush
- Rinse thoroughly
- Dry completely

**Step 6: Reassemble and season**
- Replace all parts
- Turn on high for 15 minutes
- Oil grates before next use

### Charcoal Grill Deep Clean

**Step 1: Remove all ash**
- Wait until completely cool
- Dispose of ash safely
- Check ash catcher

**Step 2: Scrub interior**
- Use putty knife on buildup
- Scrub with soapy water
- Rinse with garden hose

**Step 3: Clean grates**
- Remove and soak
- Scrub with brush
- Rinse and dry

**Step 4: Season grates**
- Apply thin coat of oil
- Heat grill to set oil
- Prevents rust

## Seasonal Maintenance

### Spring (Start of Season)

- Deep clean entire grill
- Replace worn parts
- Check gas connections
- Test igniter
- Order replacement parts

### Summer (Peak Season)

- Weekly brush cleaning
- Monthly deep clean
- Monitor for wear

### Fall (End of Season)

- Thorough deep clean
- Apply rust preventive
- Cover securely
- Store propane safely

### Winter (Off-Season)

- Keep covered
- Check periodically for pests
- Avoid using in snow/rain

## Common Problems & Solutions

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Uneven heat | Clogged burners | Clean burner ports |
| Yellow flames | Blocked air intake | Clean venturi tubes |
| Won't ignite | Dead igniter | Replace battery/igniter |
| Excessive smoke | Grease buildup | Deep clean drip area |
| Rust on grates | Moisture exposure | Season and cover |
| Flare-ups | Excess grease | Clean drip pan regularly |

## When to Replace Parts

### Grates
**Replace when:**
- Severe rust
- Bars breaking down
- Food sticks constantly

**Lifespan:** 3-5 years with care

### Burners
**Replace when:**
- Holes or cracks visible
- Uneven flame pattern
- Corroded beyond cleaning

**Lifespan:** 5-10 years

### Heat Plates
**Replace when:**
- Rusted through
- Severely warped
- Holes developing

**Lifespan:** 3-5 years

### Igniter
**Replace when:**
- No click sound
- Sparks but doesn't light
- Inconsistent ignition

**Lifespan:** 2-5 years

## Extending Grill Life

### Do's
✅ Use a quality cover
✅ Clean after every use
✅ Oil grates regularly
✅ Store propane properly
✅ Keep in dry location

### Don'ts
❌ Leave uncovered
❌ Use harsh chemicals
❌ Ignore rust spots
❌ Skip regular cleaning
❌ Store with propane attached
