## Charcoal Grills

### Pros
- **Superior flavor**: Authentic smoky taste that gas can't replicate
- **Higher temperatures**: Can reach 700¬∞F+ for perfect searing
- **Lower initial cost**: Quality charcoal grills are more affordable
- **Portability**: Great for camping and tailgating
- **Versatility**: Better for smoking and low-slow cooking

### Cons
- **Longer startup**: 15-20 minutes to heat up
- **More cleanup**: Ash disposal required
- **Less temperature control**: Requires more skill to manage heat
- **Ongoing fuel costs**: Charcoal needs regular purchasing
- **Weather dependent**: Wind affects performance

### Best For
- BBQ purists who prioritize flavor
- Those who enjoy the ritual of grilling
- Smoking and low-slow cooking enthusiasts
- Budget-conscious beginners
- Portable/camping use

## Gas Grills

### Pros
- **Instant ignition**: Ready to cook in 10 minutes
- **Precise control**: Easy temperature management
- **Consistent results**: Predictable cooking every time
- **Easy cleanup**: No ash to deal with
- **Convenient**: Perfect for weeknight dinners

### Cons
- **Less smoky flavor**: Missing authentic charcoal taste
- **Higher cost**: Quality gas grills are expensive
- **Less portable**: Heavy and require propane tanks
- **Lower max temperature**: Harder to achieve extreme heat
- **Maintenance**: More parts that can fail

### Best For
- Busy cooks who grill frequently
- Those who value convenience
- Beginners wanting consistent results
- Year-round grillers
- Large family cooking

## The Flavor Question

The biggest difference comes down to flavor. Here's why:

### Charcoal Flavor
- Smoke from burning charcoal infuses food
- Fat drippings create flavorful smoke when they hit coals
- Authentic "BBQ" taste

### Gas Grill Flavor
- Clean-burning propane produces minimal smoke
- Flavor comes mainly from searing and seasonings
- Can add smoke boxes or wood chips to enhance
